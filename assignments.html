Assignment 1: Prototype

The first prototype that we were tasked to do was to make a phone stand out of cardboard and at the sane time ensure that it is collpasable to make it more conveneient for the user to bring around
The following are the steps I took to make the final product.

STEP 1: think of a design and sketch the idea out onto a piece of paper
note: sketched on phone first because i had no paper on hand but later transferred design to a paper.



STEP 2: search for suitable cardboard to create the prototype
note: ensure that it is not too hard so that it would be easy to cut into and ensure that it is not too soft so that it can support the phone.



STEP 3: make an outline on the selected carboard to use as guide to cut in the the next step



STEP 4: follow the guide drawn in the earlier step and cut out the shape



STEP 5: assemble the prototype by joining the two pieces and test it out with a phone!!

Assignment 5: 3D Printing

The fifth assignment that we were tasked to do was to create a chess peice using Fusion360. The head of the chess piece was to be extruded and the base, revolution. The following were the steps i took to get my finished product

STEP 1: search online for an outline of the chess piece and download it

insert a picture

STEP 2: insert the outline of the downloaded chess piece in the form of a canvas

insert a picture

STEP 3: enter sketch mode and use the 'line' function to create a rough outline of the chess piece.

insert a picture

STEP 3: measure the height and width of the chess piece to ensure that it is of the appropriate size and click 'finish sketch'. Follow by extruding the head piece of the chess piece, for me i used 3mm.

insert a picture

STEP 5: next, i created an outline for half of the base piece and ensured that it was more or less centralised, after i exited the sketch mode.

insert a picture

STEP 6: after exiting sketch mode, i then used revolution on the base piece to suit the type 'full'. After, i saved the project as an STL file

insert a picture

STEP 7: i opened Cura and selected Ultimaker 2+ as the 3D printer with PLA filament. i then uploaded the chess piece into Cura and tuned the settings to ensure that i could print the chess piece within 45 minutes.

insert a picture

STEP 8: i added the gcode file into the sd card of the ultimaker 2+ to upload my design into the 3D printing machine. After selecting my design, i sent it for printing and began the waiting process.

STEP 9: After waiting about 20 minutes, my chess piece was ready to be removed and i had my very own chess piece made by me!
